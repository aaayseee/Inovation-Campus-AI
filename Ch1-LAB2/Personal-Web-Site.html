01 / Who I Am

An engineer who builds the tool instead of just the analysis.

I graduated as a double-major in Industrial and Computer Engineering from İstanbul Kültür University, ranked 2nd in my department with a 3.66 GPA and three High Honor certificates. But the degree is only half the story - what defines the way I work is a habit of turning theoretical models into things people can actually click, run, and trust.

Where a conventional industrial engineer stops at a spreadsheet or a report, I keep going: I formulate the optimization model in GAMS or MILP, wrap it in a Python or ML pipeline, and give it a face - a planning interface, a dashboard, a deployed app. This is the thread running through my internships at Turkcell, TUSAŞ, LC Waikiki, and QNB Group: four very different industries, one consistent instinct to make operations legible and software-driven.

I move comfortably between the shop floor and the codebase - between a 5S ergonomics study on an aircraft assembly line and a fine-tuned LLM pipeline for customer complaints. That range is not a lack of focus; it's the point.

03 / My Future Goal

Building the operating systems behind real-world decisions.

Every project I've listed above points in the same direction: I don't want to just analyze operations - I want to build the software that runs them. My goal is to grow into a role that sits between machine learning engineering and operations research: someone who can formulate the model, train the forecast, solve the optimization, and ship the interface that a planner actually uses.

I'm looking for opportunities in ML engineering, R&D, data analysis, and operations/logistics optimization - ideally in teams that treat AI-driven decision systems as production software, not one-off notebooks. Having worked across aerospace, telecom, retail logistics, and banking, I bring a rare comfort moving between domains, which I plan to keep using to solve harder, more consequential operational problems.

Longer term, I want the flow-shop model, the linehaul optimizer, and the complaint-resolution pipeline to be early data points in a career spent making AI-powered optimization the default way industrial problems get solved - not the exception.
